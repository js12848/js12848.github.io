The Medium is the Massage — Reading Response

Quick Take

The medium shapes our habits more than the content. This book performs that claim—bold layouts, fragments, and image–type collisions—so you feel the point instead of just reading it. It made me notice how formats train behavior: a feed teaches skimming; a printed page asks you to dwell. Picking a medium is already a design choice about tempo and attention.

  • Environment over message: we see content first, the container last.
  • Participation: jump‑cuts ask the reader to connect dots.
  • Scale/time: media compress distance and change what “presence” feels like.
