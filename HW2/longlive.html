Reflection — Long Live the Web (2010)

Berners-Lee argues the web’s superpower is openness: royalty-free standards, decentralized architecture, and permissionless linking. He warns that walled gardens and ISP interference fracture this commons. Our responsibility as builders is to publish with semantic HTML, stable URLs, and portable data so innovation doesn’t require anyone’s permission.

“The Web is critical not merely to the digital revolution but to our continued prosperity—and even our liberty.”
